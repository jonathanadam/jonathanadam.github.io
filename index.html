Me A small autobiography

I am Jonathan Adam - some call me Jay, or Jadam. Belgian by passport, via many detours, I have ended up studying a BA in computer science and music composition at Brown University (in Providence RI, USA), and am now completing an MS in Interactive Media Technology at KTH (in Stockholm, Sweden) and an MA in Musicology at Uppsala University (also in Sweden). I am currently finishing off theses on informal technologies in Cuba, and on city soundscapes focusing on street musicians and marginalized communities.

My interests span far and wide, but talk to me particularly about sound and technology, music composition, creative technology, international development, or my hopes for technology serving underprivileged communities. Starting June 2018 I will be embarking on a year-long frontend engineering internship in San Francisco at Rocket. After that, I hope to use my coding skills in yet another far-flung place, figuring out how to best use my knowledge to make the world a more equitable place.

Contact me by email, or on LinkedIn or Instagram. I also have a Soundcloud.

Projects that I have worked on

Interacting with trees

Fall2017. How can digital technology give new dimensions to outdoor play? Together with Adrian Benn and Nicola Marcon, we created a set of five interactive nodes which allow children to interact with a set of musical loops. I was primarily responsible for the Python networking code and the sound design.

Sound Forest

Spring2017. The Sound Forest instrument is a bespoke museum installation for the Scenkonstmuseet. Adrian Benn and I set out to create a sound interaction that found its own rules of engagement within the affordances of the installation. Adrian and I both worked on Max/MSP code and sound design.

Chronogram - Interaction Design Redesign

Spring2017.KTH Social, the primary digital portal for students at KTH, is due a redesign. Together with Carl Törnqvist, Adrian Benn, and Nikolaos Syropoulos, we create a Chrome extension that scrapes the page's data and pours it into a new design. Using a Chrome extension allows us to iterate through designs quickly, and makes it easier for users to test our designs in their own contexts. I was primarily responsible for interaction design sketches.

The Corporate Ladder

Fall2016.A sandbox puzzle game, in which an office worker must figure out how to exploit particular office quirks to climb up in the corporate hierarchy. Made with Alexander Lingtorp, Dan Hemgren, Ewoud Van der Heide, Nilay Ates and Rodrigo Roa Rodríguez, I was responsible for character rigging, story, and project management. More info here.

Uprooted

Fall2015. An animated short made in CS125-Intro to Computer Animation at Brown University. A tree deals with loss, a changing world and the passing of time thorughout the year. The story was a group effort with Melody Cao, Allison Hamburger and Mina Shakarsky; I was responsible for character rigging and sound design, as well as certain shots.

Music for Walking

Spring2016. My senior thesis in my Music composition concentration at Brown University, advised by Eric Nathan. Within my role as a composer, I investigate the interplay between physical movement and sound, focusing on walking. How does the personal embodied mechanics of walking build towards a perception of the world around us, and towards a larger role for pedestrians in the city? A year of investigating, composing, choreographing and directing led to a forty-five minute recital involving two dancers, six instrumentalists, and video. Audio recording available on my Soundcloud.
